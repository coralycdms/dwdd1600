Planning

Let's Get a Score of 5!

Some of the steps to make that happen are:

Be Consistent With Your Inquiry
Sticking to your theme is crucial. That is the most important aspect of your portfolio. Your portfolio is answering your inquiry/theme.
Your Skills
The artworks themselves need to demonstrate skill and effort. Skill is great, but by itself, it will not get you to a score of 5 on your AP Art portfolio.
Revision and Processes
Showcasing improvement is key to getting a high score. Sprinkling in some process pieces will work in your favor.
Time Management
You cannot grind out a well-produced painting or sculpture in one hour. Set aside time for your portfolio and avoid the embarrassment of submitting unfinished work.
Applying Feedback
Feedback for an artist is what water is to soil. It propels you forward in your art journey.
You're Like a Bird
Don’t bind yourself to a cage. Allow room for experimentation even when it feels scary.

In sum, to put yourself out there, you have to be willing to bare yourself to the world. And unlike in the real world, this will make achieving a high score more likely.

Learning Tools

Carry a Sketchbook

  1. Jot down anything that inspires you.
  2. Play around with color schemes.

Look at Art

  1. Periodically look at art from your peers and ask questions.
  2. Research famous artists and compare processes.
  3. "Everything is a remix." Inspiration is not plagiarism.
