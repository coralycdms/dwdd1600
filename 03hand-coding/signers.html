Planning

Let's get a score of 5!

Some of the steps to make that happen are:

Be Consistent With your Inquiry
Sticking to your theme is crucical. That is most impoartant aspect of your porfolio. Your portfolio is answering your question/theme.
Your Skills
The artworks themleves need to demonstrate skill and effort. Sure, skill is a great thing to have, but by itself, it will not be able to drag you to 1st place. In this case, to getting a 5 on your AP art portfolio. Does you art have variety or are you a one trick pony? Your art needs to show continuous improvement until the end.
Revision and Processes
As stated above, showcasing improvement is a key to getting a high school. Sprinkling in some proccess peices here and there will be in your favor. You can even choose to 'redo' a previous piece in your portfolio at a later date. However, do not rely on this too much. When in doubt, you've already done too many revisions! Start anew.
Time Management
As my beloved AP teacher once said, "Procrastination is a silent, but preventable, killer." That has proven to be true every day since I have heard it. You cannot grind out a well-produced painting or sculptor in 1 hour. Well, not consitentently at leas. Set apart time for your portfolio and you can avoid the embarrassment of submitting an unfinshed art work.
Applying Feedback
Yes it hurts to have a valued piece of art of yours be criticized but remember this. Feedback for an artist is what water is to soil. It is meant to propel you forward and further into you art journey.
Your like a Bird
Yes, I am indeed quoting Nelly Furtado. Don't bind yourself to a cage. Allow room for experimentation even when it feels scary. Taking risks shows you are open to growth and lessons.

In sum, to put yourself out there, you have to be willing to bare yourself to the world,
