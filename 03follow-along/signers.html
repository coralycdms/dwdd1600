Declaration of Independece

Famous Signers

Some of the most famous include:

Thomas Jefferson
Principal author of the Declaration of Independence and later the 3rd U.S. President.
John Adams
Strong advocate for independence who later became the 2nd U.S. President.
Benjamin Franklin
Celebrated statesman, diplomat, inventor, and writer.
John Hancock
President of the Continental Congress, famous for his large, bold signature.
SAMUEL Adams
Key organizer of colonial resistance and leader of the Sons of Liberty.
Richard Henry Lee
Proposed the resolution declaring the colonies independent.
Roger Sherman
The only person to sign the Declaration, the Articles of Confederation, the Continental Association, and the Constitution.
Charles Carroll of Carrollton
The only Catholic signer and one of the wealthiest men in the colonies.
Robert Morris
Major financier of the Revolutionary War.
Elbridge Gerry
Later Vice President; namesake of “gerrymandering.”

In sum, while all 56 signers played a crucial role in forging the nation's independence, a handful became especially influential through their leadership, political achievements, or lasting contributions to American identity. Their signatures represent not only personal courage but also the collective resolve that shaped the founding principles of the United States.
